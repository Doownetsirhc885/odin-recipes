Homemade Mac-n-Cheese Recipe

Macaroni and cheese is a quintessential American comfort food loved for its creamy texture and rich, cheesy flavor. It evokes a nostalgic warmth with each bite, combining tender macaroni pasta enveloped in a velvety cheese sauce that can range from mild and creamy to sharp and tangy, depending on the cheeses used. The satisfaction of indulging in mac n cheese is unparalleled; it's hearty, comforting, and instantly lifts your spirits. Making this classic dish at home is surprisingly straightforward, requiring just a handful of ingredients—pasta, cheese, milk or cream, butter, and seasonings.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces (about 2 cups) elbow macaroni or any pasta shape of your choice
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (such as sharp cheddar, Gruyère, or a blend)/li>
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper, or to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika (optional, for color and flavor)
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder (optional, for added flavor)/li>
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (optional, for topping)

How to Make Homemade Mac-n-Cheese

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Make the Cheese Sauce: In the same pot or a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Once melted, add the flour and whisk constantly for about 1 minute to create a roux. This will help thicken the sauce.
  3. Add Milk: Gradually pour in the milk while whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Cook the mixture, stirring occasionally, until it starts to simmer and thickens slightly, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add Cheese and Seasoning: Reduce the heat to low. Add the shredded cheese to the milk mixture, reserving a small amount for topping if desired. Stir until the cheese is melted and the sauce is smooth
  5. Season the Sauce: Stir in the salt, black pepper, paprika, and garlic powder, adjusting to taste.
  6. Combine Pasta and Sauce: Add the cooked pasta to the cheese sauce and stir until well combined and all the pasta is coated with the sauce.
  7. Optional Breadcrumb Topping: If using breadcrumbs, melt a tablespoon of butter in a small skillet over medium heat. Add the breadcrumbs and toast until golden brown, stirring frequently.
  8. Serve: Serve the macaroni and cheese hot, optionally topped with the toasted breadcrumbs and a sprinkle of reserved shredded cheese.
Tips: Cheese: You can use any combination of cheeses you like, but a mix of sharp cheddar and Gruyère creates a rich and flavorful sauce. Customization: Feel free to add cooked bacon, diced ham, peas, or chopped vegetables to the mac and cheese for added flavor and texture. Baking Option: If you prefer baked macaroni and cheese, transfer the mixture to a baking dish, sprinkle with breadcrumbs and extra cheese, and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 20-25 minutes until bubbly and golden on top.
