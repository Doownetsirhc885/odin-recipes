Falafel Recipe

Falafel is a beloved Middle Eastern dish consisting of small, deep-fried balls or patties made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, mixed with herbs, spices, and onion. Originating in Egypt but now enjoyed across the Middle East and globally, falafel boasts a crispy exterior with a tender, flavorful interior. The taste is savory and earthy, often enhanced by herbs like parsley and cilantro, and spices such as cumin and coriander, giving it a uniquely satisfying profile. Its popularity stems from its vegetarian-friendly nature, versatility (served in pita sandwiches, salads, or as a snack), and delightful texture. Making falafel at home is relatively simple with canned or soaked chickpeas blended with the aforementioned ingredients, formed into balls or patties, and fried until golden brown. With just a few steps and ingredients, homemade falafel offers a fresh, flavorful alternative to store-bought versions, perfect for anyone craving a taste of the Middle East.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas
  • 1 small onion, roughly chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)x
  • 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 4 tablespoons all-purpose flour or chickpea flour (for binding, if needed)
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

How to Make Falafels Step-by-step

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas: Place the dried chickpeas in a large bowl and cover them with cold water. Allow them to soak overnight (about 12 hours). The chickpeas will double in size, so make sure you have enough water and a large enough bowl.
  2. Make the Falafel Mixture: After soaking, drain the chickpeas well. Transfer them to a food processor. Add the chopped onion, parsley, cilantro, garlic, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper (if using), salt, pepper, and baking powder to the food processor.
  3. Blend the Mixture: Pulse all the ingredients together until they are finely minced. The mixture should be coarse and grainy, not a paste. Scrape down the sides of the food processor as needed to ensure even blending.
  4. Check the Consistency: Test the mixture by pressing a small amount between your fingers. It should hold together. If it's too dry, add a tablespoon or two of flour and pulse again. Be cautious not to over-process; you want a slightly coarse texture.
  5. Shape the Falafel: Once the mixture is ready, transfer it to a bowl. Using your hands, shape the mixture into small balls or patties, about 1 1/2 inches in diameter. If the mixture is too sticky, you can lightly oil your hands.
  6. Fry the Falafel: Heat vegetable oil (about 2 inches deep) in a deep skillet or pot to 350°F (175°C). Carefully place the falafel patties into the hot oil, a few at a time, without overcrowding the pan. Fry until deep golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  7. Drain and Serve: Once cooked, remove the falafel from the oil using a slotted spoon and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  8. Serve the falafel warm with pita bread, hummus, tahini sauce, and your favorite salad ingredients like lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and onions.
Notes: Chickpeas: It's important to use dried chickpeas that have been soaked, not canned chickpeas, for the right texture.
