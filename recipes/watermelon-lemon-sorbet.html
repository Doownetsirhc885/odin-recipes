Watermelon Lemon Sorbet

Sorbet is a refreshing frozen dessert known for its light, fruity flavors and smooth, icy texture. Unlike ice cream, sorbet is dairy-free, made primarily from fruit puree or juice mixed with sugar and water. This results in a texture that is intensely fruity and slightly icy, akin to a frozen fruit slush. Making sorbet at home is remarkably easy, especially in the summertime when fresh fruits are abundant. All it takes is blending together your favorite fruits (like strawberries, mangoes, or lemons) with sugar syrup and a splash of lemon juice for brightness. Once blended, simply freeze the mixture until solid, occasionally stirring to prevent large ice crystals from forming. The simplicity of sorbet lies in its minimal ingredients and no-churn method, making it a delightful and cooling treat perfect for hot summer days.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups cubed seedless watermelon (about 1 small watermelon)
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice (about 3-4 lemons)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (adjust according to the sweetness of your watermelon)
  • Zest of 1 lemon (optional, for extra lemon flavor)

How to Make Watermelon Lemon Sorbet

  1. Prepare the Watermelon: Cut the watermelon into small cubes, removing any seeds. You should have about 4 cups of cubed watermelon.
  2. Blend the Ingredients: In a blender or food processor, combine the cubed watermelon, fresh lemon juice, granulated sugar, and lemon zest (if using). Blend until smooth and the sugar is completely dissolved.
  3. Blend the Ingredients: In a blender or food processor, combine the cubed watermelon, fresh lemon juice, granulated sugar, and lemon zest (if using). Blend until smooth and the sugar is completely dissolved.
  4. Chill the Mixture: Transfer the blended mixture to a bowl or container and refrigerate for at least 1-2 hours, until thoroughly chilled. Chilling helps the sorbet freeze faster and improves the texture.
  5. Churn the Sorbet: Once chilled, pour the mixture into an ice cream maker and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions. Typically, this takes about 20-25 minutes until the sorbet reaches a soft-serve consistency.
  6. Freeze the Sorbet: Transfer the churned sorbet into a freezer-safe container. Smooth the top with a spatula, cover tightly with a lid or plastic wrap directly on the surface of the sorbet to prevent ice crystals from forming.
  7. Freeze Until Firm: Allow the sorbet to freeze for at least 4 hours or overnight until firm.
  8. Serve: Before serving, let the sorbet sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften slightly. Scoop into bowls or glasses and garnish with fresh mint leaves or lemon slices if desired.
Tips: Sweetness: Adjust the amount of sugar based on the sweetness of your watermelon and your personal preference. Variations: You can experiment with other citrus fruits like lime or orange instead of lemon for different flavor variations. Storage: Store any leftover sorbet in the freezer, covered tightly, for up to 2 weeks. Allow it to soften for a few minutes at room temperature before scooping.
